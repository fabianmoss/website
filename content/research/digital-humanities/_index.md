Digital humanities combines computational methods with traditional humanities scholarship to analyze, interpret, and visualize cultural materials. This interdisciplinary field encompasses text analysis, data mining, network analysis, geospatial mapping, and more. In musicology, digital humanities approaches enable large-scale corpus studies, interactive visualizations, and the creation of sustainable digital editions that make historical sources accessible to researchers and the public alike.
